DRAFT REPORT: Wage Theft in the Jurisdiction of Santa Clara County for WTC NAICS Industry

These data are a combination of the Department of Labor Wage and Hour Division cases (not all result in judgments), the Division of Labor Standards Enforcement judgments, The WHD data were obtained from the DOL, the DLSE data were obtained through a Section 6250 CA Public Records Act request (up to Aug 1, 2019 and does not include purged cases which are those settled and then purged typically after three years).

The dataset in this report is pulled from a larger dataset that for all regions and sources contains 578,755 cases, 5,969,449 violations, 4,395,749 employees, and $ 5,489,041,898 in backwages, and $ 203,600,336 in restituted backwages. This is approximately a 25-percent sample of an estimated actual $22B annually in wage theft that occurs nationally (see https://blog.tsheets.com/2018/news/cost-of-wage-theft).

The Federal WHD data goes back to 2000, and the State DLSE data goes back to 2000.

These data are internally incomplete, and do not include private lawsuits, stop notices, and complaints to the awarding agency, contractor, employment department, licensing board, and district attorney. Therefore, the following is a sample given the above data constraints and the reluctance by populations to file wage and hour claims.

Notes and methods summary

Report generated 08/27/2023

Based on open source software prepared by the Center for Integrated Facility Engineering (CIFE) at Stanford University in collaboration with the Santa Clara County Wage Theft Coalition. These data have not been audited and, therefore, are only intended as an indication of wage theft.

Notes:

(1) In the tables and city summaries, the zip ####X represents data that is missing the zip code field. (2) There are unlabeled industries, many of these are actually construction, care homes, restaurants, etc. just there is not an ability to label them as such--a label of 'other' could lead one to indicate that they are not these industries and therefore the category of 'undefined.' (3) Values may deviate by 10% within the report for camparable subcategories: this is due to labeling and relabeling of industry that may overwrite a previous industry label (for example Nail Hamburger could be labeled service or food).

Note that categorizations are based on both documented data and intelligent inferences, therefore, there are errors. For the fields used to prepare this report, please see https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/19EPT9QlUgemOZBiGMrtwutbR8XyKwnrEhB5rZpZqM98/edit?usp=sharing . And for the industry categories, which are given shortened names here, please see https://www.naics.com/search/ . To see a visualization of the data by zip code and industry, please see (last updated Feb 2020) https://public.tableau.com/profile/forest.peterson#!/vizhome/Santa_Clara_County_Wage_Theft/SantaClaraCounty .

The DIR DLSE uses one case per employee while the DOL WHD combines employeees into one case.

Methods:

backwage_owed:

  • the sum of wages owed, monetary penalty, and interest
  • df['backwage_owed'] = df['wages_owed'] + df['cmp_assd_cnt'] + df['interest_owed']

wages_owed:

  • unpaid backwages less payment recieved by employee
  • df['wages_owed'] = df['bw_amt'] - df['ee_pmt_recv']

interest_owed:

  • interestd due less interest payments recieved
  • df['interest_owed'] = df['Interest_Balance_Due'] - df['Interest_Payments_Recd])

interest_balance_due:

  • where interest balance due is missing, then infer an interest balance based on a calaculated compounded interest of the backwages owed
  • df['Interest_Balance_Due'] = np.where(df['Interest_Balance_Due'] == , df['Interest_Accrued'], df['Interest_Balance_Due']
  • df['Interest_Accrued'] = (df['wages_owed'] * (((1 + ((r/100.0)/n)) ** (n*df['Years_Unpaid']))) ) - df['wages_owed']
